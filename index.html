Recent research projects

M dwarf ages in the CMD

I collected a sample of 74216 M and L dwarfs with Halpha measurments and Gaia photometry, parallaxes and proper motions. I used this sample to study age indicators in the color-magnitude diagram.

Read more

The sample contains M and L dwarfs. It is from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey and I cross-matched it with Gaia DR2. This sample contains spectral types, radial velocities, Halpha equivalent widths from SDSS and proper motions and parallaxes from Gaia DR2. I modified the quality cuts suggested by the Gaia Collaboration to fit later M and L dwarfs. Find more about the sample in my paper here. Download the sample from Zenodo. And reproduce this color-magnitude diagram and more with this jupyter notebook m-dwarfs-gaia-cmd.

Age-activity relation for M dwarfs

I characterized the Halpha Age-Activity relation for M-dwarfs using a sample of age-indicators with Halpha equivalent widths. The ages were obtained from known moving groups and white dwarfs co-movers.

Read more

I collected a sample of M-dwarfs which have Halpha equivalent widths from the literature. To obtain ages, I identified which belonged to known moving groups and which had a wide white dwarf companion. Using this sample, I was able to calibrate the Halpha age-activity relation. Read my paper to learn more about this work.

Ages of white dwarfs

To estimate ages of white dwarfs I developed an open soruce python package called wdwarfdate. This code estimates cooling and total ages from an effective temperature and a surface gravity. The source of wdwarfdate can be found in GitHub and I also wrote online documentation with tutorials on how to use the code.

Read more

wdwarfdate uses a Bayesian inference to obtain an age probability dstribution. The paper to cite this code is in prep.

Complex Networks applied to Social Phenomena

From March 2016 to August 2016 I worked on a project in statistical physics under the supervision of Prof. Claudio Dorso. I used the Axelrod method to study the opinion formation in a social network. The goal of the project was to study the influence of a few agents that were against vaccines, in a society in favor of vaccines. We modeled the society as a 2D complex network where each agent was a node with certain characteristics. My contribution to the project was to add a global agent that interacted with all the agents, which could represent a journal or the TV.

High Energy Particle Physics

From February 2015 to March 2016, for my Licenciatura thesis (master equivalent) I worked on a project in Particle Physics under the supervision of Prof. Daniel de Florian. For my thesis project I calculated a sixth order correction to the Lagragian of the Standard Model to include the production of two Higgs particles from two gluons. Find the slides of my thesis defense here.

Calcium signals in frog egg cells

From March 2014 to December 2014 I worked on an experimental project in Biophysics under the supervision of Prof. Silvina Ponce Dawson to measure calcium signals from frog egg cells. We used a confocal microscope to observe the calcium signals that we stimulated with UV light. We designed a devise to be able to tune the UV light emitted and study the reactions of the cell to different light signals. Find a talk in spanish about the project here.

Outreach

During my undergraduate studies, I joined the outreach group at the University of Buenos Aires where I participated in different events to take science to the public such as: “Physics week” and “Museum Night”. During these events we had exhibitions with physics experiments for middle and high school students, and the general public. I also participated in monthly meetings with groups of high school students to explain to them what the Physics career is about and what it means to be a scientist.

Furthermore, as a member of the American Museum of Natural History (AMNH) Astronomy department, I had several key opportunities to interact with middle and high school students participating in museum outreach events. These included speaking with small visiting classes from ESL (English as a second language) schools and running an astronomy booth for local visiting families at an all-Spanish planetarium program.
