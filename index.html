Sample with age indicators

To infer ages of low mass stars, we need a big sample with age indicators. That's why I compiled a sample of 74,216 M and L dwarfs from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey and cross-matched it with Gaia DR2. This sample contains spectral types, radial velocities, Halpha equivalent widths from SDSS and proper motions and parallaxes from Gaia DR2. I modified the quality cuts suggested by the Gaia Collaboration to fit later M and L dwarfs. The Figure to the right shows the color-magnitude sample color-coded with spectral type.

Find more about the sample in my paper here.
And download the sample from Zenodo.

Past Research

During and after my undegraduate studies, I did projects in different areas inside Physics. The three following projects were done at The University of Buenos Aires, Argentina.

Complex Networks applied to Social Phenomena

From March 2016 to August 2016 I worked on a project in statistical physics under the supervision of Prof. Claudio Dorso. I used the Axelrod method to study the opinion formation in a social network. The goal of the project was to study the influence of a few agents that were against vaccines, in a society in favor of vaccines. We modeled the society as a 2D complex network where each agent was a node with certain characteristics. My contribution to the project was to add a global agent that interacted with all the agents, which could represent a journal or the TV.

High Energy Particle Physics

From February 2015 to March 2016, for my Licenciatura thesis (master equivalent) I worked on a project in Particle Physics under the supervision of Prof. Daniel de Florian. For my thesis project I calculated a sixth order correction to the Lagragian of the Standard Model to include the production of two Higgs particles from two gluons. Find the slides of my thesis defense here.

Calcium signals in frog egg cells

From March 2014 to December 2014 I worked on an experimental project in Biophysics under the supervision of Prof. Silvina Ponce Dawson to measure calcium signals from frog egg cells. We used a confocal microscope to observe the calcium signals that we stimulated with UV light. We designed a devise to be able to tune the UV light emitted and study the reactions of the cell to different light signals. Find a talk in spanish about the project here.
