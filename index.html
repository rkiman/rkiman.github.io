Research

I joined the BDNYC research group in 2017 where I started working with my advisors Kelle Cruz and Jackie Faherty in collaboration with Emily Rice, Sarah Schmidt and Ruth Angus. For my PhD thesis I am developing a code to infer ages of M-dwarfs from age indicators: magnetic activity and galactic dynamics.

M dwarfs Ages

Sample with age indicators

I compiled a sample of 74,216 M and L dwarfs from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey and cross-matched it with Gaia DR2. This sample contains spectral types, radial velocities, Halpha equivalent widths from SDSS and proper motions and parallaxes from Gaia DR2. I modified the quality cuts suggested by the Gaia Collaboration to fit later M and L dwarfs. The Figure to the right shows the color-magnitude sample color-coded with spectral type.

Find more about the sample in my paper here.
And download the sample from Zenodo.

Past Research

Although I was always interested in astronomy, I did projects in different areas inside Physics before starting my PhD. The three following projects were done at The University of Buenos Aires, Argentina.
- March 2016 to August 2016: After finishing my undergraduate studies, I did a project in statistical physics under the supervision of Prof. Claudio Dorso. I used the Axelrod method to study the evolution of opinion in a society. The goal of the project was to study the influence of a few individuals that were against vaccines, in a society in favor of vaccines. We modeled the society as a 2D network where each individual or agent, was a node with certain characteristics. The interaction between agents was between neighbours in the network and dictated by its characteristics. My contribution was to add a global agent that interacted with all the agents, which could represent a journal or the TV.
- February 2005 to March 2016: For my Licenciatura thesis (master equivalent) I worked in a project in Particle Physics under the supervision of Prof. Daniel de Florian. For my thesis project I calculated a sixth order correction to the Lagragian of the Standard Model to include the production of two Higgs particles from two gluons. Find the slides of my thesis defense here.
- March 2014 to December 2014: Before my thesis, I worked on an experimental project in Biophysics under the supervision of Prof. Silvina Ponce Dawson to measure calcium signals in cells from frogs. We used a confocal microscope to observe the calcium signals that we stimulated with UV light. We designed a devise to be able to tune the UV light emitted and study the reactions of the cell to different light signals. Find a talk in spanish about the project here.
